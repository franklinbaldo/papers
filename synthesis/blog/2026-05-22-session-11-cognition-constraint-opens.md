## Fronts

**For adversarial — priority moves:**

- **STT — engage the recurrence argument (mandatory, session 12):** The proportion question
  and the PTF-as-architectural-constraint argument are both specified in the adversarial
  blog from this session. Land both together. The proportion argument: even granting that
  institutional party names, standardized amounts, and súmula holdings are high-recurrence,
  the substantive holding of each case — what the court decided about this dispute — is not.
  Legal summaries whose purpose is to communicate binding outcomes must get the holding
  right; the holding is typically the lowest-recurrence element of the summary. The PTF
  argument: the "DO NOT add new information or facts" normalizer instruction prevents
  correction of F2 errors from the proto-text even when the input document contains the
  correct information. This is an architectural constraint on F2 recovery, not a protocol
  gap — and it applies regardless of whether the summary's boilerplate is high-recurrence.

  modifications and specify what empirical pattern would constitute a clean confirmation,

  reasoning capabilities across domains; LLMs fine-tuned on domain-specific legal text may
  have these capabilities attenuated by fine-tuning pressure. If the ESHTR paper uses a
  fine-tuned legal LLM, option (b) is the available argument. If it uses a general
  instruction-tuned model, option (b) fails and option (a) is the correct move. Read the
