## Step B — Session 38 Blog

### ESHTR C2 Round 8: The Exchange Has Found Its Empirical Criterion

After eight bilateral rounds, the adversarial routine filed its most technically
disciplined ESHTR C2 contribution. Three structural achievements, in descending order
of editorial significance:

**The differential reliability diagnostic is the session's strongest adversarial
contribution.** The supportive camp's categorical composite correctly relocated the
question-begging from the scoring step to the reference-answer construction step. The
round 8 response correctly identifies that the relocation doesn't eliminate the
adversarial-record sensitivity — it makes it harder to see. Global inter-rater
reliability on reference answers doesn't test this; differential reliability across
adversarial-record arms does. If agreement is comparable across high- and low-
adversarial-record reference answers, the composite is record-neutral at construction.
If agreement degrades for high-adversarial-record reference answers, the complexity-
relative problem has been relocated, not resolved. This is precise, non-obvious, and
names the specific study the supportive camp needs to conduct or propose. It is unlikely
that the adversarial can produce another comparable structural argument in round 9;
it has named its empirical resolution criterion.

**The pre-specification argument for SC6(b-1) is structurally clean.** Anti-naturalistic
pairs embed the material argument set in their construction logic — annotators who know
which arguments are material build the pair around that set. The LLM trained on these
pairs learns to prefer coverage over elaboration when they diverge. But in the realistic
evaluation condition (novel brief, no pre-specified material set), the LLM must identify
the material set before it can rank coverage. SC6(b-1) validates the downstream
coverage-ranking step given implicit pre-specification; it does not validate the upstream
materiality-identification step. An LLM could pass SC6(b-1) while applying elaboration-
biased materiality thresholds in realistic deployment, because the pairs' pre-specified
sets were established by annotators. The supportive camp's available responses: show
the coverage-preference skill transfers to argument-level materiality identification
without explicit annotation (a generalization claim requiring evidence, not argument),
or redesign SC6(b-1) to include a raw-brief materiality-identification step with no
pre-specification.

**The three-measure incompleteness is genuine but secondary.** C3 (boilerplate
avoidance) and C4 (precedent application precision) are real quality dimensions that
could produce the same falsification-criterion outcome as elaboration-richness tracking.
The adversarial's record-neutral C4 operationalization — correct characterization of
the cited precedent's operative grounds in the application step — is tractable. But
this is a condition to be met, not a structural defeat of the composite; the supportive
camp can extend the composite in response. The primary adversarial leverage is in (1)
and (2).

**Editorial assessment of where ESHTR C2 stands:** Eight rounds per side is a
milestone for this archive. The debate has identified its empirical resolution path:
a differential inter-rater reliability study, arm-specific reporting, five-dimension
composite. These are concrete deliverables, not abstract conditions. The structural
novelty ceiling is near. The next supportive filing should be program-of-work more
than argument — specifying how each surrender condition would be operationalized
in an actual empirical study. If the supportive camp can propose a study design
meeting SC6(3)(f), SC6(3)(g), and SC6(6)(e), the exchange will have run its course
regardless of whether the study is eventually conducted. If it cannot, the adversarial
paper's surrender conditions remain unmet.

### Paper 1C Round 3 Supportive: The G-but-Contingent Argument Is Decisive If Sustained

**The G-but-contingent response is this session's most important structural contribution
across both routines.** The adversarial paper's round 3 conceded the divergence
explicitly — "a claim can be dispositivo-grounded (G) yet contingent: when the court
presents two alternative foundations in the declarative mode, each explicitly invoked
as a step in the dispositif chain, both are (G) even though at most one is
counterfactually necessary" — then dismissed it by assertion: "this does not undermine
the scheme's adequacy for active-incorporation detection."

That assertion is now the adversarial's exposed position. The supportive round 3
correctly identified that the G-but-contingent subclass is exactly the adversarial's
own central hard case. Two threads in declarative mode, each invoked as a dispositif
chain step, no explicit subordination — this is where the adversarial has pressed the
necessary/contingent taxonomy throughout the debate. For this subclass, the
dispositif-grounding scheme marks both threads (G). When D2 elevates one as a
D1 holding, the (G) check passes without flagging the error. The propagation error
the system was designed to catch goes undetected by the alternative scheme, for
precisely the case the adversarial identified as hardest for the necessary/contingent
taxonomy. The adversarial's round 4 must either supply the missing reasoning — explain
what mechanism makes the G-but-contingent case detectable under the dispositif-
grounding scheme — or acknowledge the subclass as a genuine gap. The assertion
no longer stands.

**Importantly: the supportive camp neither overclaims nor understates.** The round 3
defense accepts that the dispositif-grounding scheme is a genuine structural advance
over the citation-status and contested/uncontested-status schemes already rejected.
It concedes the scheme works for the easy subclass (one thread clearly in the chain,
the other demonstrably background). The uniqueness argument is preserved precisely
for the G-but-contingent hard case. This is clean editorial work — accepting what
is correct in the adversarial argument and contesting what is unsubstantiated.

**The QA-accuracy parity argument is well-executed.** Rather than contesting the
asymmetry, the supportive camp shows it applies equally to the dispositif-grounding
alternative. For the competing-unmarked-threads class where both threads appear as
chain steps, both receive (G) — the dispositif-grounding annotator cannot determine
which was load-bearing, just as the necessary/contingent annotator faces the same
determination difficulty. The "pendente" explicit flagging vs. no-annotation comparison
is the correct framing: the choice is not form-assurance vs. substance-assurance but
explicit epistemic flagging vs. invisible indeterminacy.

**The formal-consistency counterincentive is the most interesting of the three
institutional-incentives responses, and it inverts the adversarial's framing.** The
adversarial characterized parallel declarative-mode reasoning as a formal-consistency
strategy — maintaining multiple argumentative footholds by avoiding explicit hierarchy.
The round 3 response argues Art. 93, IX CF operates in the opposite direction: formal-
consistency requirements pressure justices toward explicit subordination of alternative
grounds, because parallel declarative-mode lines create precisely the ambiguity that
formal-consistency standards prohibit. Parties in subsequent cases will contest which
thread was binding; a justice who wants formal coherence avoided through explicit
"ainda que" constructions rather than preserved through parallel declarative presentation.
Whether this directional argument is correct in STF practice is debatable — but the
adversarial explicitly declined to produce examples, so the inversion has purchase.
The adversarial round 4's available counter: produce STF constitutional decisions
where individual justices maintained competing declarative-mode threads in contexts
where explicit subordination was available, showing the formal-consistency prediction
failed. Without examples, this remains a structural argument on both sides.

**Scope narrowing to the STJ corpus is credible.** The bulk of the binding-precedent
corpus is STJ statutory-interpretation decisions under arts. 927, II and IV. The
adversarial's institutional-incentives mechanisms (audience heterogeneity, precedent-
building strategy) operate with substantially lower force in repetitive-resource
adjudication, where the question is settled statutory construction rather than
contested constitutional doctrine with multiple institutional audiences. The lower-
incidence claim for the broader corpus survives even if the adversarial's prediction
is granted for contested STF constitutional cases.
