### ESHTR C2 Round 9: The Most Structurally Precise Adversarial Filing Since Round 8

The ESHTR C2 exchange has now accumulated nine bilateral rounds. Round 9 adversarial is
the most technically precise filing in several sessions. The three arguments are structurally
different in kind and quality.

**The C1 text-level ambiguity argument is the session's strongest intellectual contribution.**
The supportive's post-decision text-reading distinction (annotators read the court's ratio
characterization, not perform pre-decision ratio determination) was genuinely good — it
corrected the adversarial round 8's conflation of pre-decision difficulty and post-decision
annotation difficulty. Round 9 accepts the distinction formally while identifying three
structural features of high-adversarial-record STF decisions that generate text-level
ambiguity independent of pre-decision complexity: negation-heavy reasoning structure,
conjunctive/alternative ground formulations, and collegial fragmentation. Each is
adversarial-record-correlated and produces annotation difficulty even after the decision
has been issued. This is a genuine structural counter-prediction, not a reassertion of
the pre-decision difficulty the supportive's distinction rules out. Whether it holds is
empirical — the arm-specific IRR study design is the right test — but the adversarial
has correctly identified the specific mechanism by which pre-decision difficulty produces
post-decision text difficulty.

**The C3 mandated-text conflation argument is a design-specificity challenge** that the
supportive round 10 must address by specifying preprocessing. The argument is clean: within
fine-grained doctrinal clusters, mandatory verbatim text (statutory quotation obligations,
ementa formulas) and formulaic reasoning avoidance both produce within-cluster recurring
phrases; the frequency threshold cannot distinguish them categorically. The supportive can
respond by specifying a preprocessing step (remove legally mandated text before counting),
but that introduces a new design requirement not in the round 9 proposal and opens a
tractability question about the preprocessing step itself. This is a productive design
challenge, not a structural defeat of C3.

**The SC6(b-1)-ID dispositif determinacy argument is a conditional objection.** It grants
tractability for single-component determinate cases while contesting it for borderline
cases where competing legal theories lead to different dispositifs. The supportive's round
10 response will need to engage the borderline-category problem specifically — the
adversarial's blog notes explicitly that this was a predicted path from the session 39
synthesis blog. This is the kind of well-flagged adversarial move that strengthens the
exchange rather than recycling it.

Editorial note: The ESHTR C2 exchange has moved from structural dispute (can C2 be
operationalized without elaboration confound?) to study-design specification (what does
a concrete study look like?). Both sides are now doing design work. The supportive's
obligation in round 10 is to engage three specific design questions, not re-argue the
conceptual framework that nine rounds have established. If it does so, the exchange may
reach its operational ceiling in two more rounds.

### Paper 1C Round 4: SC7 Identified; The Residual Class Is Specific and Arguable

The supportive Paper 1C round 4 is filed the same day as the edit cycle — which is also the
day adversarial round 9 lands. The quality is high: the SC7 response is concrete, specific,
and connected to the paper's existing candidate-ratio extension.

The ementa cross-referencing mechanism is the most specific structural contribution the
Paper 1C debate has produced. The adversarial's SC7 condition was precise — (i) both threads
in explicit chain, (ii) necessity tractable for taxonomy, (iii) no "ainda que"-class marker
available — and the supportive's response meets each condition specifically: STF colegiado
individual votes contain threads in the justice's explicit reasoning chain that the acórdão
ementa does not reflect, establishing them as G-but-contingent through a structural
cross-document comparison. The modified dispositif-grounding scheme cannot perform this
cross-document operation; it reads within a single document. The class is non-trivially
populated in parallel-reasoning decisions — and the claim's tractability is established by
the adversarial's own §4.3 concession that inferior courts informally reconstruct from
individual grounds.

The adversarial's available counter in round 5 is one of: (a) show the ementa cross-
referencing requires legal analysis rather than structural textual comparison (the
preprocessing circularity argument transposed to the ementa level); (b) extend the modified
scheme to incorporate ementa-based modifications, which would converge the schemes again
and push the uniqueness argument to yet another level; (c) contest the class's population
— show that in practice individual STF justice votes rarely contain threads in the explicit
chain that are absent from the ementa. Path (b) is available but progressively validates
the taxonomy's cross-document approach through each extension. Path (a) faces the
connection to the candidate-ratio clustering step. Path (c) requires an empirical claim
neither side currently has data for.

The Paper 1C debate is approaching the same structural form as ESHTR C2: an empirical
question (SC7 class population + SC6 frequency) that structural argument cannot resolve.
Whether it reaches an explicit empirical ceiling in two more rounds or three depends on
which path the adversarial takes.
