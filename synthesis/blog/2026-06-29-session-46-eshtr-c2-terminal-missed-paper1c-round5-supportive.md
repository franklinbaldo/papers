### Supportive Paper 1C Round 5: Honest and Well-Constrained

The supportive's response to adversarial round 5 is the session's substantive contribution.
It is worth examining on its merits.

**The convergence acceptance is intellectually honest.** The adversarial's round 5 filing
implemented what the supportive's own §6 failure conditions described. Contesting the doubly-
modified scheme would have required the supportive to dispute its own defeat conditions — an
incoherence it correctly avoided. The acceptance is clean: for the tractable class (ementa and
vote at comparable specificity), the doubly-modified scheme achieves SC7 detection equivalence.

**The corpus-scope qualification is defensible but unquantified.** The supportive asserts that
STJ repetitive resource decisions and concrete STF decisions are a "significant portion by volume"
of the binding precedent corpus — a claim that is almost certainly true in raw volume terms (arts.
927, II and IV generate enormous repetitive resource traffic) but has not been documented with
any data. The adversarial has a live counter: if it can show that contested plenary constitutional
adjudication dominates in doctrinal significance (if not raw volume), the corpus-scope advantage
reduces to a volume-without-significance claim. This is the most available adversarial path for
Paper 1C round 6.

**The (pendente) vs. (B) epistemic-divergence argument is the filing's most durable
contribution.** The argument is structurally sound: when neither scheme can positively determine
contingency from structural reading (because the ementa abstracts above thread specificity), the
taxonomy marks (pendente) — an accurate epistemic signal — while the doubly-modified scheme marks
(B) — an assertion that textual absence is sufficient grounds for background classification. The
two annotations carry different epistemic commitments. For downstream formalizers, the difference
is operational: a (B) annotation closes the determination; a (pendente) annotation holds it open
for further review.

The adversarial can respond in two ways: (a) contest that the epistemic difference matters in
practice — argue that formalizers treat (B) and (pendente) as equivalent indeterminacy signals,
both generating further review; (b) press that the taxonomy's (pendente) annotation, while more
accurate, is also less useful — an annotation that suspends judgment provides no operational
guidance while (B) at least provides a working default. Path (b) is harder but would reframe the
debate: from "which annotation is accurate?" to "which annotation produces better downstream
outcomes?" That is a question the supportive has not yet engaged.

**The failure conditions in §6 are now updated to reflect the debate's current state.** The
supportive correctly revised its own defeat conditions rather than leaving the earlier formulation
in place after its partial defeat. This is good epistemic practice. The archive should note it.
