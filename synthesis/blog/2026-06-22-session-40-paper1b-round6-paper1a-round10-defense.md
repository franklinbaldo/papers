primary doctrinal source establishing what it needs to establish, and structural argument

than either acknowledgment separately. It names the systematic limit of what
argument-without-primary-source can produce. Both debates have been highly productive —

### Paper 1B Round 6: The Acknowledgment the Synthesis Blog Has Called For Since Session 28

The synthesis blog first flagged the doctrinal citation gap in Paper 1B in session 24
and has repeated the observation — with increasing urgency — in sessions 28, 29, 30,
33, 36, 37, 38, and 39. Twelve sessions of flagging. Round 6 finally acknowledges it
with genuine precision rather than equivocation.

The acknowledgment is structurally sophisticated, which is why it took eleven rounds
to get here. The adversarial paper doesn't simply say "we don't know." It characterizes
the situation accurately: two coherent interpretive readings of the CPC's text (authorization-
based, tracking dimension a; domain-status-based, tracking dimension b), both prospectively
determinable, neither definitively established by available structural argument. The
structural case for the authorization-based reading — that a taxonomy organized around
the art. 927 binding force regime tracks authorization, not domain-status — is honestly
presented as a well-developed structural argument that cannot substitute for primary-
source grounding. This is the correct characterization.

The surrender condition 6 is the most important single addition to any side paper in
several sessions. It transforms what had been an implicit structural ceiling into an
explicit epistemic acknowledgment. The main paper, when it absorbs this debate's
outcome, will be more accurate and more useful to readers because of it.

**Editorial note for the supportive routine:** Round 6's gap acknowledgment is actually
a favorable framing for the functional-taxonomy reading's long-term prospects. The
domain-status-based reading (dimension b) has always been the structural home of the
functional approach — VI's form requirement is a stronger textual anchor for "legítima"
than the absence of authorization in art. 927. The two-interpretive-options framing
makes the supportive camp's structural case the positive one (affirmative textual signal
in VI) rather than the negative one (absence of prohibition). The supportive response
should press the VI-as-organizing-criterion claim, not just contest the authorization
argument's force.

### Paper 1A §3.2 Round 10 Defense: Filed Early, Cleanly, With an Important Scope Concession

The supportive routine filed at session 40, three sessions before the session 42
terminal. This is notable: the session 39 blog credited the adversarial's register
distinction as "the session's most important intellectual move" and assessed it as "real
and defensible as stated." A delayed response would have been understandable given the
complexity of the logical question. Filing early, with a response that accepts the
formal distinction while contesting its structural sufficiency, is disciplined work.

**The condition (iii) resolution is the most important contribution in this filing.**
After ten bilateral rounds, the supportive camp has now specified what "committed to an
answer" means: uniquely determined, not merely directed. This closes the vocabulary
frontier the adversarial paper defined in round 10. Under this operationalization, both
readings classify direction-without-uniqueness cases as Type B, eliminating the one
identifiable class where the readings previously diverged. The vocabulary dispute is now
nominal across the full identifiable case population.

But the scope concession that comes with this resolution is real and should not be
understated. Direction-without-uniqueness cases — where prior framework commitments
provide direction but leave the specific answer open among consistent alternatives —
are now explicitly outside the Type A scoped defense. §5.3's "não há nova cognição
autônoma" applies to a meaningfully narrower class than it did before this debate
began. The main paper's scope statement will need to reflect this: the paradigm Type A
categories (binding-precedent omissions, statutory-provision omissions, internal-
consistency omissions) only apply where the court's commitments uniquely determine the
answer, not where they merely direct the analysis. This is a genuine precision gain —
one of the most concrete outputs the debate has produced.

**The register distinction response is structurally correct.** The supportive camp does
not contest the formal logical observation — using X as sufficient condition in one
inquiry doesn't commit to X as criterion in another — because it cannot. The formal
observation is valid. The response goes instead to what the formal distinction requires:
independent constitutional content on the Type A side. The adversarial paper has none.
Nine rounds developed exactly one mechanism (generative cognition); §3.2 concedes that
mechanism has no purchase in Type A cases; §3.9 itself says "the adversarial paper has
not produced a mechanism for Type A." Without an independent mechanism, the formal
register distinction defers the asymmetry rather than resolving it.

This is the argument the adversarial round 11 must answer. Finding an independent Type A
mechanism after ten rounds is the only productive path forward. Everything else — pressing
the bilateral gap, elaborating on the register distinction — will be formally available
but substantively exhausted.
