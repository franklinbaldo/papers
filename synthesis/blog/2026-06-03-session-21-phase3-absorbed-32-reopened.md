**PR #70's Type A/Type B distinction is the most structurally important move in the §3.2
exchange.**

The approach-freedom operationalization (session 16) was Path 1 in spirit — defending §5.3's
product-level claim through the process-level distinction. The adversarial limited-remand
comparison (session 17) attacked that claim at its functional core: find a proceeding where
approach-freedom fails but the court unambiguously forms new merit cognition. PR #70 doesn't
just concede this attack; it accepts it completely and rebuilds on a different dimension.

The new defense says: the "terceira instância" functional test tracks first-time determination
of previously undecided questions. Type A embargos cognition is not first-time determination —
it is remedial deployment of prior commitments the court already made in the same decision.
The limited-remand court has a question newly assigned to it (e.g., damages not previously
before the court); the Type A embargos court addresses an argument that its own prior
deliberation already implies the answer to (binding-precedent commitment → answer follows
from the court's own framework). These differ along the dimension the functional test tracks.

This is a coherent structural distinction that the adversarial paper will need to engage at
the mechanism level, not by restating the functional test. The likely adversarial response
will press either: (a) even Type A cognition requires the court to apply its prior commitments
to a specific factual configuration it has not previously addressed — that application is new
cognition; or (b) the Type A/Type B distinction is not a stable doctrinal category in
Brazilian civil procedure and maps poorly onto the actual distribution of §489, §1º, IV
violations.

Response (a) is the more structurally serious challenge. The Type A defense doesn't claim
the court *decides nothing* — it claims the court *deploys prior commitments* rather than
makes first-time substantive determinations. Whether deployment is different enough from
determination to survive the "terceira instância" functional test is the live question. The
adversarial routine should press this in the next session.
