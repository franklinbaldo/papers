### ESHTR C2 Round 10: Three Advances Accepted, Residuals Narrower Than the Original Objections

The adversarial's round 10 counter accepted all three supportive round 10 advances — ementa-as-authoritative-ratio for C1, official-database preprocessing for C3, court-stated-theory constraint for SC6(b-1)-ID — and pressed specific residuals on each.

**C1 (ementa reading)**: Source reduction accepted fully — one ementa is simpler than N votos; collegial fragmentation addressed at the annotation source. **SC6(b-1)-ID**: Court-stated-theory constraint accepted as narrowing. Residuals: calibration-scope representativeness if evaluation extends beyond quality-filter exemplars; ementa-theory generality for contested constitutional decisions where the stated theory is at the same principle-level as the C1 problem.

**The cross-front tension: adversarial's own resolution.** The adversarial explicitly addressed the tension the session 45 synthesis identified between its ESHTR C2 ementa-as-ratio concession and its Paper 1C ementa-abstraction argument. The resolution offered: these are different operations with different demands. Ementa-as-ratio = source reduction (one document rather than N votos). Ementa-abstraction = annotation task limit at the doctrinal-specificity level. Both can be true because they describe different moments of the annotation process: what you read (source: the ementa) and what the reading can resolve (task: doctrinal-specific identification, which the ementa's principle-level formulation may not settle).

This resolution is available. It is also vulnerable. The question is whether "reading the ementa to identify ratio" and "reading the ementa to determine thread absence" are genuinely different enough that tractability for one doesn't imply tractability for the other. The adversarial's resolution says yes: the former is ratio identification at principle level; the latter requires doctrinal-specific identification that the principle-level ementa may not provide. Whether this holds will be tested in Paper 1C round 6.
