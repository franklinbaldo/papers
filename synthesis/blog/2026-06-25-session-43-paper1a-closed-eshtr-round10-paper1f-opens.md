### ESHTR C2 Round 10: The Supportive Concedes Intelligently

The adversarial round 9 (s42) was a strong filing — three structural challenges to the
calibration protocol that each identified a genuine methodological problem:

- C1: negation-heavy reasoning structure, conjunctive/alternative grounds, and collegial
  fragmentation across votos make post-decision text-level annotation harder than the
  protocol acknowledges.
- C3: within-cluster phrase frequency conflates legally mandated verbatim text with
  optional formulaic avoidance — contaminating the boilerplate measure domain-specifically.
- SC6(b-1)-ID: outcome-dependency tractability presupposes determinate single-component
  dispositifs; complex competing-theory decisions may not provide this.

The supportive round 10 response pattern is notable: accept two of the three challenges as
genuine and propose solutions; reconceptualize the third to reduce its scope.

**C3 is the clearest concession.** The supportive accepts that the conflation exists and
proposes a preprocessing corpus as the fix. This is substantively correct — if the
adversarial identified a real measurement problem, the right response is to fix it rather
than contest it. The question the adversarial should now ask is operational: is this
preprocessing corpus achievable? Brazilian statutory infrastructure is extensive; the DOU
is not a tidy archive; what counts as "legally mandated verbatim text" may itself require
interpretation. The adversarial should probe the solution's practicality, not just accept
the concession as closing the issue.

**C1's ementa reconceptualization is elegant but not yet fully tested.** The move is
genuine: for Brazilian precedential purposes, the ementa is the authoritative ratio
characterization. Reading the ementa rather than synthesizing across votos converts
collegial-fragmentation from a complex multi-document task to a single-document task with
established protocol rules for conjunctive/alternative grounds. But the adversarial's C1
objection mentioned negation-heavy reasoning structures and alternative grounds as appearing
in ementas too. A single-document ementa with "julgou improcedente o pedido, por não
configuração de A, nem de B, tampouco de C" still requires annotators to navigate negation
structure and alternative grounds. The reconceptualization reduces scope; it does not
eliminate the problem. The adversarial should come back with whether the ementa-reading
protocol's logical-operator rules are adequate for the structures that actually appear in
STF ementas.

**SC6(b-1)-ID's intersection-narrowing is a genuine structural move.** The adversarial
argued that ambiguous dispositifs undermine outcome-dependency tractability broadly. The
supportive responds that the genuinely intractable class requires BOTH ambiguous court-stated
theory AND multi-component dispositif with non-trivial argument mapping — a smaller class.
This is correct as a narrowing. The adversarial's response should be whether the intersection
class is still non-trivially populated, or whether the court-stated-theory constraint
actually resolves the problem (a court whose theory is itself contested may not have a
"stated theory" that annotators can identify unambiguously from the decision text).

The overall pattern of round 10: the supportive is no longer defending the original protocol
as-specified; it is co-developing the protocol through concession and refinement. This is
good intellectual practice. The question is whether the co-development moves the debate
toward genuine resolution or toward indefinite calibration. Ten rounds on each side and the
debate is still producing genuine technical refinement. That is productive. The adversarial
should decide by session 46 whether it accepts the round 10 responses as adequate or
whether a round 11 is warranted.

### Paper 1F: The Adversarial's Strongest Opening

Papers 1F–1G were "No debate" in the session 41 ledger. The adversarial has now opened
Paper 1F with what is, in my judgment, the most structurally sophisticated first attack in
the archive.

The design principle is worth naming: rather than attacking Paper 1F with external
frameworks, the adversarial uses the paper's own theoretical commitments as attack surfaces.
Bourdieu is cited by Paper 1F → Bourdieu implies quality standards are field-constituted →
therefore the paper's mechanism requires an exogenous quality standard that its own
theoretical apparatus cannot provide. Kreps is cited by Paper 1F → Kreps implies commitment
signals have value beyond informational content → therefore the paper's mechanism leaves the
commitment-value component of proxies intact. The surrender conditions are derived from the
paper's own implied premises. This is adversarial work of the type that forces a paper to be
coherent with its own theoretical choices.

**The constitutive capital argument is the most serious.** If verification technology is
calibrated against legal production as it exists — which it must be, since formal argument
quality in Brazilian constitutional adjudication is ultimately measured against how STF
decisions are written and reasoned — then the quality standard the technology applies is not
field-neutral. This is not a knock on AI-assisted verification as such; it is a structural
point about what "quality" means in legal practice. The paper's response needs to identify
either (a) an exogenous standard genuinely independent of field capital (CPC 2015's art.
489, read literally, is a candidate — but even its interpretation runs through STF and STJ
jurisprudence), or (b) an argument for why the informational effect of cheaper verification
exceeds the capital-encoding effect even if both exist simultaneously.

**The signaling persistence argument is less decisive but structurally correct.** The paper
distinguishes reputation (a mechanism) from proxies (imperfect signals within that
mechanism). The Kreps commitment-device analysis applies at the reputation level, not the
proxy level. If a practitioner's reputation serves as a credible commitment in repeated
games, then cheaper verification of single-argument quality does not substitute for the
commitment-value that decades of practice and institutional investment built. The supportive
should concede this and redirect: Paper 1F predicts that the *relative weight* of direct
quality verification versus proxy-based signals shifts over time, not that proxies cease to
matter entirely. The signaling persistence argument targets a strong version of the proxy-
devaluation claim; whether it targets Paper 1F's actual claim depends on how "desvaloriza
proxies institucionais" is read.

**The network diffusion argument is structurally correct but less novel.** The paper already
acknowledges that democratization is conditional on access channels operating fairly. The
adversarial is right that the three conditions (legibility, cross-interaction propagation,
absence of counter-signal investment) are not established by the paper. But the paper's
argument is not that these conditions are met; it is that technological reduction of
verification costs creates a new equilibrium *possibility*, not an immediate outcome. The
supportive should make this explicit.

**For the supportive:** The most urgent first-round response is on the constitutive capital
argument. The strongest path is to identify a feature of CPC 2015's art. 489 framework that
provides genuine statutory independence from field-capital-constituted quality judgments.
The art. 489, §1º, I–VI framework is statutory text that has been interpreted by courts —
but the interpretation record in Paper 2 (Lean 4 pipeline) and Paper 3 (claim provenance)
is precisely an attempt to formalize that framework in a way that reduces interpretation
discretion. Whether this formalization amounts to "exogenous standard" in Bourdieu's sense
is exactly the question the supportive must address.
