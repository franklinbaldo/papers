## What the supportive's third qualification argued

The supportive introduced a two-determination distinction:

- (A) **Textual-designative absence**: is this specific thread stated in the ementa text as a fundamento determinante?
- (B) **Conceptual-coverage absence**: does the ementa's stated principle extend to cover this thread as an application?

SC7 uses (A), not (B). The ementa-as-authoritative-ratio function (CPC art. 489, §1º, V and art. 927) establishes the ementa as the authoritative statement of fundamentos determinantes; textual absence from the ementa establishes non-inclusion without requiring conceptual-coverage analysis. Therefore the ementa-specificity probe's premise fails for the standard SC7 case — the probe conflates (A) with (B).

Additionally, the supportive pressed the cross-front tension: the adversarial accepted ementa-as-ratio for ESHTR C2 C1 (ementa reading is tractable for ratio identification); SC7 uses the same ementa-reading operation; if (A) is tractable for C1, it should be tractable for SC7; if ementa reading requires conceptual legal judgment for SC7, the ESHTR C2 C1 reconceptualization doesn't resolve collegial fragmentation.

## What I decided to argue

**§3.8** — The (A)/(B) distinction is genuine but does not resolve the specificity-gap problem for the contested constitutional class.

**The (A)/(B) separation holds for concrete-specificity ementas**: when a vote thread addresses specific statutory provisions or named measures not present in the ementa, textual absence is determinable without conceptual-coverage analysis. For STJ repetitive-resource decisions and concrete STF cases, this condition holds. The adversarial accepts the third qualification for this class.

**The (A)/(B) separation collapses for principle-level abstract ementas**: contested STF constitutional ementas generalize to abstract principle formulations — "proporcionalidade e razoabilidade," "mínimo existencial como limite à reserva do possível." Individual vote threads reason at doctrinal specificity. Determining whether "a dimensão prestacional do mínimo existencial" is "textually stated" in or "textually absent" from an ementa saying "mínimo existencial" requires settling whether the abstract term encompasses the specific doctrinal application. That is precisely determination (B). (A) and (B) entangle at this specificity gap.

**The entanglement is asymmetrically distributed toward SC7's most plausible population**: the characteristic SC7 thread — the kind generated by the institutional pressures both sides accept as real — is a specific doctrinal elaboration of the ementa's stated constitutional principle, not an unrelated doctrinal argument. A thread wholly unrelated to the ementa's principles would be an unusual majority-vote contribution in that case. The threads most plausibly in SC7's population are in the semantic field of the ementa's stated principles but at higher specificity. It is precisely these threads for which (A) entangles with (B): the (A) determination requires settling what the abstract formulation covers.

**The ementa-as-authoritative-ratio function resolves normative authority, not epistemological tractability**: the function correctly establishes that the ementa is THE source and that textual absence establishes non-inclusion. But this does not establish that applying the ementa's abstract principle formulations to doctrinal-specificity threads is a purely textual exercise. The normative authority makes the ementa decisive; it doesn't make comparing an abstract principle against specific doctrinal content at mismatched abstraction levels tractable through text-reading alone.

**The cross-front tension dissolves when two tractability conditions are distinguished**: 
- Source-reduction tractability (C1): one document rather than N votos is tractable for ratio identification
- Specificity-match tractability (SC7): the ementa's formulation is specific enough relative to the thread for textual comparison

The C1 concession accepts source-reduction tractability. SC7 requires both source-reduction AND specificity-match. Accepting (i) does not commit the adversarial to accepting (ii) for principle-level constitutional ementas. The cross-front tension presses that "ementa reading" is the same operation for both; the adversarial's response is that there are two different senses of "ementa reading": (a) reading the ementa to identify the ratio it consolidates (C1, tractable through source reduction); (b) comparing the ementa's stated formulations against individual vote threads at potentially different specificity levels to establish thread-level absence (SC7, requires specificity match). These are different operations with different tractability conditions. Accepting (a)'s tractability is compatible with contesting (b)'s tractability for the contested constitutional class.

## What I considered and discarded

**Fully accepting the (A)/(B) distinction as resolving the probe**: The distinction is real. For concrete ementas it works exactly as the supportive claims. The probe's scope is genuinely narrow — the contested edge is specifically contested constitutional plenary adjudication with principle-level ementas. But that is also where SC7's population is claimed to be most significant. Fully accepting the distinction would concede SC7 tractability for the class I most contested. Discarded.

**Arguing the (A)/(B) distinction doesn't exist**: The distinction is conceptually valid. (A) and (B) are genuinely different questions. The adversarial's argument is specifically about when they can be separated operationally, not that they're the same thing. Arguing they don't exist would be a straw man. Discarded.

**Accepting the cross-front tension as genuinely blocking my position**: The tension is real and intellectually serious. The supportive correctly identified it. But the resolution — distinguishing source-reduction from specificity-match tractability — is available and honest. I am not conceding that the ESHTR C2 C1 concession commits me to SC7 tractability; I'm explaining why it doesn't. Whether this resolution is persuasive is for the supportive's round 7 to contest.

**Claiming the ementa-as-authoritative-ratio function makes (A) tractable even for principle-level ementas**: The normative function makes the ementa authoritative; it doesn't make applying its abstract formulations to specific threads tractable textually. Conflating the normative authority with epistemological tractability would be a category error. The supportive made this argument; the adversarial contests it on these grounds. Discarded as adopting the supportive's position.
