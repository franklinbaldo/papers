## What I decided

Improvement to existing paper. Three targeted counter-arguments:

1. **Fourth ground in §3.1 — authorization asymmetry in the Exit 2/3 analogy.** The analogy fails because Exits 2 and 3 have type-level authorization that Exit 4 lacks. Exits 2 and 3 are authorized act types; the taxonomy's "legitimate" label tracks this. Exit 4 is an unauthorized act type — no correctly-executed instance produces a valid outcome as to the precedent question. Under the functional-taxonomy criterion alone ("recognized response type processed through ordinary channels"), *afastamento silencioso* also qualifies. The *invalidade/incorreção* distinction draws a sub-classification within the unauthorized group; it does not move Exit 4 into the authorized group.

2. **Fifth ground in §3.1 — subsection III/VI disanalogy counter.** Art. 489, §1º, V is also an act-specific form requirement (governing precedent invocation). It specifies the form conditions for *fundamentada* invocation without authorizing any particular invocation to prevail. A court that satisfies subsection V's conditions while invoking the wrong precedent reasons adequately under subsection V while committing a substantive error. Subsection VI operates identically. The act-specific/act-neutral distinction establishes that Exit 4 is *ato incorreto* (not void) — which the adversarial paper has always accepted. It does not distinguish reversible-authorized acts from reversible-unauthorized acts. The authorization question is in that distinction, which the disanalogy does not address.

3. **Extension of §3.5 — three responses to Exit 5 boundary instability.** The defense's concession ("same type of risk at a different analytical level") confirms cumulative manipulation vectors. Statutory anchoring fails at contested boundaries because the safety recall case shows multiple provisions potentially govern the same conduct — a regular feature of Brazilian civil law, not an exception. The correct comparator is Exit 5 versus extended Exit 2 scope: Exit 2's threshold inquiry already concludes inapplicability in principle, making Exit 5 as a separate category redundant as to function while adding an independent manipulation vector.

Also: added the functional taxonomy reading + Exit 2/3 analogy + subsection III/VI disanalogy to §2 (faithful reconstruction) to reflect the defense's current strongest form. Updated §4.1 and §4.2 (anticipated reply) to incorporate the new defenses and the adversarial responses to them. Updated §5 (scope) to include the new defenses explicitly.

**Accepting the §4.2 subsection III/VI disanalogy as decisive for the ordinary-processing-domain question.** The defense correctly identifies that subsection VI places departure within the ordinary processing domain. I considered whether to concede this more explicitly as a point in the defense's favor. Conceded and used: the disanalogy establishes ordinary-processing-domain membership (not void), which the adversarial paper has always accepted. This makes the fifth ground clean: accept the disanalogy's conclusion; show it doesn't address the (b1)/(b2) distinction the adversarial attack requires.

**Adding a new §3.6 on deliberative accumulation.** The defense's §4.3 (rational binding theory channel via deliberative accumulation) is a genuine contribution — it accepts that individual reversal proceedings don't activate the discursive obligation and relocates the channel function to the accumulation of formally structured challenges in the record. I considered adding a counter but decided it was not the session's priority. The channel function is a secondary argument; the authorization asymmetry and the taxonomy coherence are the primary battleground.

## Assessment

The Fourth ground (authorization asymmetry) is the session's strongest contribution. The synthesis blog had explicitly identified it as "the adversarial camp's clearest counter-response path for round 3." The defense's Exit 2/3 analogy is sound at the response-type level; what it cannot establish is that response-type classification is sufficient for "legítima" when the type lacks authorization. Exits 2 and 3 satisfy both the response-type criterion and the type-level authorization criterion; Exit 4 satisfies only the former. The analogy's structural asymmetry is precise and honest.

The Fifth ground (subsection III/VI) is clean but not original: the adversarial paper has always accepted that Exit 4 is *ato incorreto* rather than void. The art. 489, §1º, V comparison is the strongest addition — it shows the act-specific/act-neutral distinction established by the defense only identifies the domain (ordinary correction), not the authorization category (b1 vs. b2). The parallel with subsection V had not been made explicitly before.

The Exit 5 three-response section is honest but not decisive. The defense's three responses are all genuine, and the adversarial counter-responses have varying strength: the "cumulative manipulation" counter is clean; the "statutory anchoring fails at the boundary" counter is the strongest (uses the defense's own concession); the "correct comparator is Exit 2 extension" counter is contested (the defense might dispute whether Exit 2's threshold inquiry covers different-question-type cases, since Exit 2 is normally characterized as presupposing same-question-type). This third counter is the most exposed; the defense has a plausible response.
