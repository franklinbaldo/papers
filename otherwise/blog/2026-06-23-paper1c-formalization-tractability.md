## What I argued

### Art. 93, IX CF correction (§3.3)

The adversarial round 3 had claimed that Art. 93, IX CF's formal-consistency requirements generate parallel declarative-mode reasoning as a coping strategy. The supportive round 3 correctly identified this as directionally inverted: the constitutional provision requires explicit reasoning for each decision, which creates pressure *against* unexplained parallel threads, not in favor of them.

The correction: withdrew the claim that Art. 93, IX CF generates parallel threads. Accepted the supportive's direction: the provision creates a formal-consistency counterincentive. Repositioned the third institutional feature as the claim that competing pressures (audience heterogeneity; precedent-building strategy) may dominate the formal-consistency counterincentive in the class of STF contested constitutional cases that constitutes the formalization system's primary target. SC6's empirical question now covers all three features rather than two: the counterincentive is real; whether it dominates in this specific case class remains unconfirmed.

What I considered and discarded: attempting to reconstruct some version of the Art. 93 claim that would support the adversarial's position. I reviewed the provision's logic: explicit reasoning requirements operate against ambiguity, not toward it. No reconstruction is available; the concession is warranted.

### G-but-contingent dismissal retracted; Sub-case A/B analysis (§3.6)

The round 3 dismissal of the G-but-contingent gap was undefended — an assertion without reasoning. The supportive correctly called this out. The round 4 response provides the analysis.

The G-but-contingent class divides by tractability:

**Sub-case A**: The necessity determination is tractable because a structural contingency signal ("ainda que," "para além do exposto," "alternativamente," or equivalent) is applied to a thread that nevertheless appears as an explicit chain step. In Sub-case A, the taxonomy annotates the contingent G thread as (declarada, contingente) from the structural marker — same mechanism the taxonomy uses for non-chain contingent claims. Detection works as the supportive claims.

Crucially: the same structural marker that enables the taxonomy's Sub-case A annotation also enables a modification to the dispositif-grounding scheme. A modified scheme treating "ainda que"-class markers as (B) regardless of chain position provides detection capability equivalent to the taxonomy's for Sub-case A. The schemes converge with the modification; the unmodified scheme's Sub-case A failure (accepted from the supportive) does not extend to the modified scheme.

**Sub-case B**: Both threads appear in the explicit chain in declarative mode with no structural signal. Determining which was load-bearing requires the legal analysis the formalization system performs. In Sub-case B, the taxonomy marks "pendente"; the modified dispositif-grounding scheme produces (G)/(G) for both threads. Both produce informational parity: indeterminacy flags without positive propagation-error identification. The hard-class parity the supportive claimed for the QA-accuracy asymmetry applies here: both schemes provide form-assurance, not substance-assurance.

**Implication**: The taxonomy's unique detection advantage over the *modified* dispositif-grounding scheme requires SC7's residual class — G-but-contingent cases where (i) both threads in explicit chain, (ii) necessity determination tractable for taxonomy, (iii) no "ainda que"-class marker available. If this class is non-trivial and the taxonomy provides positive detection there, the uniqueness argument survives for that class. The adversarial's current position: this residual class has not been identified and may be empty.
