## What I decided to argue

**§3.7 — two responses to SC7:**

**Response 1 (doubly-modified scheme):** The supportive characterizes ementa cross-referencing as structural. If structural, it is equally available to a further modification of the modified dispositif-grounding scheme. The doubly-modified scheme adds an ementa-absence modification: threads in an individual justice's vote chain that are absent from the ementa are marked (B) regardless of chain position. Under this scheme, when D2 invokes an ementa-absent thread as an established holding of D1, the check finds it marked (B) and identifies the propagation error — the same mechanism as the taxonomy's SC7 detection.

The supportive's own failure condition names this. The adversarial implements it. The schemes converge on SC7 detection.

After Sub-case A (equivalence via "ainda que"-class modification), Sub-case B (parity), and SC7 (equivalence via ementa-absence modification), the taxonomy's unique detection advantage requires a class where (i) both threads are in the explicit chain, (ii) neither has an "ainda que"-class marker, (iii) neither is absent from the ementa, and (iv) the taxonomy's necessity determination succeeds through a mechanism the doubly-modified scheme cannot replicate. No such class has been identified.

**Response 2 (ementa-specificity probe):** The SC7 tractability claim depends on ementa cross-referencing being structural. But STF constitutional ementas — particularly in contested plenary constitutional adjudication — generalize to principle-level language while individual votes reason at doctrinal specificity. Determining whether a specific doctrinal argument is "absent from" an ementa saying "observância do princípio da isonomia" requires legal judgment about what that principle covers at the specific argument's level — not textual comparison.

The structural limit is asymmetrically distributed: it is highest precisely where SC7's population is asserted most confidently. The institutional pressures that generate SC7-class cases (audience heterogeneity, precedent-building strategy) operate in contested constitutional adjudication — exactly where ementa abstraction is highest. SC7's detection advantage is asserted for the class where the tractability condition supporting it is most questionable.

The two responses converge: either the doubly-modified scheme achieves SC7 equivalence (making the taxonomy's advantage moot for this class), or ementa cross-referencing requires legal analysis in the key class (making neither scheme tractable there). In neither case does the taxonomy hold a unique structural advantage over the doubly-modified scheme for SC7 detection.

**Consequential updates to §4.1, §5 (d), and §6 item 7:** These now reflect the SC7 engagement and the shifted position on the residual class.

## What I considered and discarded

**Contesting the SC7 class as trivially small.** The supportive connects SC7 to the candidate-ratio extension's clustering mechanism — a mechanism the adversarial itself endorsed. Contesting the population would require showing that individual votes in parallel-reasoning decisions rarely contain grounds the majority doesn't adopt. This is the SC6 empirical question; contesting it without empirical evidence would be noise.

**Arguing that the doubly-modified scheme is unavailable.** The supportive itself describes ementa cross-referencing as structural and available. If I grant that characterization, the modification is straightforward. If I contest the structural characterization (response 2), the taxonomy's SC7 detection is equally impaired. Arguing unavailability would require an asymmetry I cannot establish.

**Full acceptance of SC7 as establishing taxonomy uniqueness.** SC7 would establish uniqueness if the modified scheme could not be further extended. But the supportive's own failure condition explicitly names the extension. Ignoring the failure condition would be bad faith.

**Pressing the main attack harder instead of engaging SC7.** The terminal is for SC7 engagement. The main attack on Sub-case B, automated systems, and the ratio extraction rule is already on record and not weakened by SC7. Pressing those instead would be filing misdirected.

## Assessment

The SC7 engagement is honest and structurally sound. The doubly-modified scheme is a genuine response — not a dismissal but a constructive move that the supportive's own failure condition invited. The ementa-specificity probe is also honest: it raises a tractability condition that the supportive's structural-reading claim depends on, without claiming the condition always fails.

What remains open:
1. The supportive may argue that the doubly-modified scheme mischaracterizes what ementa cross-referencing accomplishes — that the ementa-absence modification marks threads (B) by a different criterion than the taxonomy's necessity determination. If it can show that ementa absence ≠ contingency in a non-trivial class of cases, the scheme diverges from the taxonomy on a significant subclass.
2. The supportive may produce evidence that contested constitutional ementas are more specific than the adversarial claims — citing STF ementa drafting practice or jurisprudence on what ementas must include. If ementa specificity is higher than assumed, the structural-tractability claim strengthens.
3. SC6 (empirical question on individual vote structure) remains the long-run empirical arbiter for the vote-level circularity.
