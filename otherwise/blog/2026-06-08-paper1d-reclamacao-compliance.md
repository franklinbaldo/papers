## What the supportive defense argued

The defense accepted the compliance proceeding's constitutional purpose as authority preservation and accepted that formal revision of a súmula requires separate procedures. It pressed three main vectors:

**§4.1 (Level 1/Level 2 denial-case):** Two arguments. First: for novel features — the characteristic class of genuinely contested reclamações — no "established scope" is available to bound Level 1 assessment; the adversarial paper's own concession that "coherence assessment in hard cases involves some examination of the súmula's ratio" concedes the point. Second (structural): the Level 1/Level 2 deferral mechanism cannot operate in denial cases because there is no downstream reversal proceeding for the scope question within the reclamação framework. The adversarial paper cannot simultaneously concede that distinction denials produce "scoping clarifications" and maintain Level 1 boundedness.

**§4.2 (Art. 927, II/Art. 926):** Art. 927, II binding force runs to the súmula as authoritatively determined. The binding obligation therefore extends to following the súmula as the STF has determined its scope. An inferior court applying the súmula to features the STF has held fall outside its scope misapplies the súmula itself — art. 927, II violation. Art. 926 independently requires consistent jurisprudência with the STF's established scope positions.

**§4.3 (Circularity):** The circularity argument restates the Thesis 2 dispute. It holds only if the compliance/merit distinction is correct — making the circularity objection a consequence of defeating Thesis 2, not an independent argument against Thesis 3.

## What changed

**§3.2 (Form Adequacy and Substantive Correctness):** Extended with three new paragraphs addressing the denial-case structural argument. Core moves: (a) The Level 1/Level 2 framework does not require the Level 2 proceeding to occur within the reclamação — it requires the substantive merit question to be addressed in the proceeding designed for merit review: ordinary appellate review of the inferior court's underlying decision. The compliance denial does not foreclose ordinary appellate routes; the Level 2 question is preserved for them. (b) The "scoping clarification" that distinction denials produce is Level 1 output: it communicates what types of argument are formally adequate distinction attempts for this súmula (what argument-types satisfy the form requirement), not whether the inferior court's substantive scope claim was correct. Level 1 scoping and Level 2 scoping are structurally distinct outputs.

**§3.4 (The Distinction Mechanism Does Not Generate Binding Precedent Under Art. 927):** Extended with five new paragraphs. First paragraph: circularity reframing accepted — the circularity observation is not pressed as independent force; it identifies the dependency structure between Thesis 2 and Thesis 3. Remaining paragraphs: art. 927, II participation inference critique (the inference that scope holdings about a listed source inherit the source's mandatory-observance status would nullify the enumeration's limiting function); res judicata vs. mandatory-observance distinction (reclamação denials produce res judicata for the parties, not mandatory-observance for future inferior courts); art. 926 consistency obligation as quality standard (generates consistency duties, not mandatory-observance elevation).

## What I considered and discarded

**Pressing the Level 2 argument at the novel-feature level directly.** The defense's §4.1 first argument is that for novel features, Level 1 can't consult established scope. I could have responded by arguing that type-level assessment (is this feature of the type the ratio could track?) is available for novel features without a full scope determination — the synthesis blog's option (b). I chose option (a) instead (the ordinary-appellate-route response) because it directly answers the structural challenge without requiring fine-grained analysis of what "Level 1 coherence" means for genuinely novel features. The ordinary-appellate-route argument is cleaner and does not require resolving the novel-feature question. If the ordinary-appellate-route argument succeeds, the denial-case structural argument fails regardless of what Level 1 requires for novel features. Including both arguments would be defensible; the paper takes the cleaner single path.

**Citing specific STF/STJ holdings on reclamação-denial binding effect.** The synthesis blog noted this is "a factual-doctrinal question with an answer in the case law." No specific citations are available without inventory. The structural argument (participation inference nullifies enumeration's limiting function; res judicata vs. mandatory-observance distinction) is sound without citations. If the supportive routine responds with specific STF or STJ decisions treating reclamação-denial scope holdings as mandatory-observance for future inferior courts, that would be the empirical answer — and it would constitute genuine progress toward SC4.

**Contesting art. 926's scope more aggressively.** I could have argued that art. 926 applies only to each court's own internal consistency, not to consistency with superior-court holdings in other cases. The provision's text does not obviously compel this narrow reading. The better argument accepts that art. 926 generates some consistency obligation toward STF reclamação-denial holdings and presses that this obligation is qualitatively different from mandatory observance — the lesser duty persuasive authority generates.

**Updating the §3.8 connection to Paper 1B.** §3.8 identifies Paper 1D's Thesis 3 as the systemic justification for Paper 1B's Exit 4. The defense's §4.6 (practice cannot establish constitutional understanding) doesn't directly affect §3.8's argument. Left unchanged.

## Assessment

The three adversarial advances are structurally sound.

On the denial-case argument (§3.2/§4.3 extension): The ordinary-appellate-route response correctly locates the Level 2 proceeding outside the reclamação framework. The defense's argument that "no reversal proceeding follows" within the reclamação is true; the adversarial response is that this is irrelevant to whether the Level 1/Level 2 distinction operates. Even in cases where no further appellate route is available on the underlying case (e.g., pure state-law cases with no constitutional question for an RE), the distinction denial communicates only Level 1 information — the departure argument was formally adequate — not that the inferior court's substantive scope claim was correct. These are different propositions, and only the former follows from a compliance determination.

On the art. 927/art. 926 response (§3.4/§4.4 extension): The participation inference critique is the strongest structural argument available without citations. If the inference held, the art. 927 enumeration would be an ineffective limiting device for STF case-by-case application decisions — a consequence the CPC 2015's careful enumeration structure contradicts. The res judicata/mandatory-observance distinction is standard doctrine. The art. 926 analysis correctly identifies the difference between the consistency obligation the defense establishes and the mandatory-observance obligation the structural-channel claim requires.

On circularity (§3.4 extension): The reframing is accepted. The adversarial case rests on the compliance/merit distinction in §§3.1–3.3; the circularity is a consequence of that attack succeeding, not an independent basis for rejecting Thesis 3. Accepting this framing is honest.

The paper reads as a coherent whole. Geological layers from earlier exchanges are not visible.
