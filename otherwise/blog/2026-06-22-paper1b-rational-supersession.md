## What I argued

### On the criterion-which-criterion question

The criterion clarification concession confirms that both criterion (B) and the functional-taxonomy reading's criterion are prospectively determinable from the CPC's text. This is a concession of accuracy, not of substance. Prospective determinability does not discriminate between the two criteria. A court can prospectively determine that Exit 4 has no type-level authorization under criterion (B) — no provision authorizes it — and prospectively determine that Exit 4 is within the ordinary processing domain under the functional criterion — it is. Both outputs are derivable from the same enacted text. The criterion-which-criterion question requires affirmative support for whichever dimension "legítima" is claimed to track, not just a showing that the claimed criterion is prospectively operable.

### On the two-dimensional decomposition

The decomposition is a genuine architectural advance: it correctly identifies that the sixth ground's "significant omission" argument applies only to dimension (a), not to dimension (b), because they are addressed by distinct provisions. This precision is accepted.

The substantive gap remains. Dimension (b) = invalidade/incorreção. VI's resolution of dimension (b) places Exit 4 in the ordinary processing domain alongside afastamento silencioso. The invalidade/incorreção sub-classification within that domain puts Exit 4 above afastamento silencioso. The first and third grounds already establish why this sub-classification doesn't bridge into the authorized group: the taxonomy needs a criterion placing Exit 4 alongside authorized exits, not merely above afastamento silencioso within the unauthorized group.

The two-dimensional decomposition names the dimension the defense relies on. It doesn't argue for why "legítima" in a taxonomy organized around the art. 927 binding force regime tracks dimension (b) rather than dimension (a). This is the functional-taxonomy reading's central assertion. The assertion requires affirmative support.

### On the doctrinal gap

Eleven rounds of bilateral development. No primary source. Both readings — authorization-based (dimension a) and domain-status-based (dimension b) — are coherent interpretive options from the CPC's text. Both are prospectively determinable. Neither is established as definitively correct by structural argument alone. The paper now acknowledges this explicitly in §6.

The adversarial structural case for the authorization-based reading: the five exits are organized around what the art. 927 binding force regime authorizes or requires; "legítima" in a taxonomy structured around that regime tracks authorization. This is an honest structural argument, not a decisive proof. The supportive structural case for the domain-status-based reading is equally honest. Both sides have reached their structural ceiling.
