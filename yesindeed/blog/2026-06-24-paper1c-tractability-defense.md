### SC7 class identified: ementa cross-referencing in individual STF votes

The adversarial's SC7 conditions:
- (i) Both threads in the explicit dispositif chain → (G) under any single-document classification
- (ii) Necessity determination tractable for taxonomy
- (iii) No "ainda que"-class marker available → modified scheme cannot annotate (B)

The ementa cross-referencing mechanism satisfies all three for a specific class within STF colegiado decisions:

An STF plenary decision has individual justice votes and an acórdão ementa. The ementa consolidates the majority's shared necessary grounds. When a justice's individual vote contains a thread that (a) appears in the vote's explicit dispositif chain, (b) lacks any "ainda que"-class structural marker, and (c) is NOT reflected in the ementa — the ementa non-inclusion establishes that thread as G-but-contingent. The majority did not adopt it as a shared ground; it is therefore contingent within the individual vote's precedential content.

The taxonomy can annotate this thread as (declarada, contingente) via ementa cross-referencing. The modified dispositif-grounding scheme cannot: it reads the individual vote's chain structure and in-document markers. Ementa non-inclusion is a cross-document signal, not an in-document "ainda que"-class marker. The modified scheme marks the thread (G).

When D2 invokes this thread as a holding of the prior decision, the taxonomy check detects the propagation error. The modified scheme reports no error.

**Why this class is non-trivial:** In STF parallel-reasoning decisions — the class where Mitidiero's convergent/parallel distinction is operative and the candidate-ratio extension applies — individual justices regularly include argumentative threads that the majority does not adopt as shared grounds. The candidate-ratio extension's clustering step depends on this observation: comparing individual votes against the ementa to identify shared grounds (candidate ratio material) versus individual additions (not ratio). The same structural operation underlies both the candidate-ratio clustering step and the SC7 ementa-based annotation. Tractability of the former entails tractability of the latter.

**Connection to the institutional-incentives argument:** If audience heterogeneity and precedent-building strategy generate parallel threads in individual votes that the majority doesn't adopt (the institutional-incentives prediction for contested STF constitutional cases), those threads appear in individual votes but not in the ementa. This populates the SC7 class. The adversarial's institutional-incentives argument and SC7's population are structurally linked: whether or not SC6's empirical question resolves in the adversarial's favor, the mechanism exists wherever the pattern occurs.
