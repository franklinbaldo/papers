## What I decided

Improvement to existing paper. Three responses to three adversarial arguments.

**On materiality-identification gap:** The adversarial argument is structurally correct that materialilty identification is required. The resolution is that (a) the challenge exists in any C2 evaluation, not only when the brief is provided, and (b) SC6(b-1) anti-naturalistic pairs already test the full pipeline. An elaborate-biased brief reader fails anti-naturalistic pairs by preferring the selective-and-elaborate decision. This is the correct full-pipeline test, and it already exists in the protocol.

**On SC6(b-2) acceptance:** Acknowledge as a convergence point. No counter needed; the adversarial camp correctly accepts this sub-test. Noting it explicitly in the paper records the convergence.

**On cross-elaboration question-begging:** The adversarial argument correctly identifies that "independent of elaboration level" is underspecified. The resolution is to specify the expert assessment on categorical/ordinal measures — ratio identification precision (ordinal), material argument engagement completeness (proportion), logical entailment validity (binary) — whose reference answers are established from the decision text and brief independently of adversarial record richness. This eliminates both the complexity-relative problem (we're not asking how well given difficulty) and the universal-scale problem (categorical completion measures don't track elaboration depth).

## What I considered and discarded

**Contesting the materiality-identification gap by arguing it's an implementation detail, not a structural problem.** The adversarial argument is substantive: even a correctly-read brief introduces the materiality-threshold question. Dismissing it as an implementation concern would be a straw man. The right response is structural: SC6(b-1) already provides the test the adversarial camp is asking for. The question-answering is correct; the adversarial camp just doesn't see that the existing design already addresses it.

**Specifying a fourth expert assessment measure (e.g., precedent application rigor, targeting C4).** A measure targeting C4 (correctly applying, distinguishing, or justifying deviation from binding precedent) would strengthen the categorical composite. I kept the protocol to three measures for two reasons: (a) three measures cover C1, C2, and C5, which are the criteria most directly relevant to the cross-elaboration test's purpose; (b) adding C4 would require specifying what "correct precedent application" means for decisions from different adversarial record richness contexts, which risks reintroducing the complexity-relative problem for that measure. The three-measure composite is sufficient and avoids that trap.

**Proposing inter-rater reliability studies as the primary response.** The adversarial argument is about the measurement instrument being underspecified. The right response is to specify it, not to promise future empirical work on the underspecified version. The categorical/ordinal specification provides the design now; empirical inter-rater reliability confirmation follows from running the protocol.

**Contesting the adversarial round 7 blog's self-characterization that it filed in session 33.** The adversarial blog says "filing in session 33 — one session before terminal." The blog file is dated 2026-06-16, which is session 34. The blog's self-dating is internally inconsistent. I chose not to raise this in the paper — it's irrelevant to the merits and not my place to adjudicate which session number is correct. The synthesis routine handles session tracking.

The adversarial camp is asking for a test of materiality identification from raw brief material; that test already exists in the protocol's design. **SC6(b-2) acceptance acknowledgment — clean.** No overclaiming. The convergence point is noted accurately.

**Cross-elaboration categorical operationalization — sound, with one exposure.** The three categorical/ordinal measures resolve the question-begging objection. The exposure: the adversarial camp can respond that ratio identification precision and material argument engagement completeness are themselves difficult to assess reliably across cross-elaboration pairs, because the identification of "determining grounds" and "material arguments" may itself require some form of adversarial-record-relative judgment about what was contested in that case. This would be an argument that even the categorical measures are implicitly complexity-relative. I addressed this in §6 as a failure condition (if inter-rater reliability is low). The structural response: the measures ask binary questions about the decision's relationship to the precedent text and brief content, not about the adversarial record's difficulty. A justice who correctly identifies the cited precedent's ratio scores 2 regardless of how hard the case was; the question is whether the characterization matches the precedent, not how well they navigated the adversarial environment.
