## What I found

The probe argues: determining whether a specific doctrinal thread is "absent from" a principle-level ementa (e.g., "observância do princípio da isonomia") requires legal judgment about what the stated principle covers at the thread's specificity level.

My round 5 accepted this as applying to the contested constitutional class. But the probe conflates two distinct determinations:

(A) **Textual-designative absence**: is this specific thread stated in the ementa text as a fundamento determinante?  
(B) **Conceptual-coverage absence**: does the ementa's stated principle extend to cover this thread as an application?

SC7 uses (A), not (B). The ementa-as-authoritative-ratio function — grounded in CPC art. 489, §1º, V and art. 927 — establishes that the ementa IS the authoritative statement of fundamentos determinantes. A thread not in the ementa text is not a stated fundamento determinante. Textually absent = not the ratio. No conceptual judgment about what the principle covers.

The probe's premise holds for (B). It does not hold for (A) — and SC7 requires (A).

**The cross-front tension**: the adversarial accepted the ementa-as-ratio framing in ESHTR C2 (the C1 concession: ementa reading is tractable for ratio identification). If ementa reading is tractable for ratio identification, it's tractable for SC7 absence determination — through the same operation. The adversarial cannot consistently hold: (ESHTR C2) ementa reading is tractable; and (Paper 1C) ementa reading requires conceptual legal judgment about coverage. If both are true, the ESHTR C2 C1 reconceptualization doesn't solve the collegial-fragmentation problem; if only the former is true, the probe fails for the standard SC7 case.

## What I decided

Third qualification added to §3.2. The probe's effective scope narrows to the edge where even the textual-designative determination requires prior conceptual judgment — i.e., where the ementa's formulation is so abstractly stated that determining whether specific thread content is mentioned requires interpreting the stated principle's scope. This is real at extreme constitutional abstraction. It does not characterize the standard SC7 case, where specific provisions, parties, or doctrinal constructions are textually absent from the ementa.

## What I considered and discarded

**Full reversal of the probe acceptance.** The probe does identify a genuine tractability problem at extreme constitutional abstraction. Fully reversing round 5 would be bad faith. The third qualification narrows the probe's scope without reversing the core concession that an edge exists. Discarded.

**Claiming (c) establishes unique taxonomy advantage over the doubly-modified scheme for the standard SC7 case.** Both schemes benefit from tractable textual-designative determination — the doubly-modified scheme also uses textual ementa absence as its (B)-marking criterion. The advantage from (c) is shared: both schemes' SC7 mechanism is tractable for the standard case. The taxonomy's unique position vis-à-vis the doubly-modified scheme remains epistemic divergence (b), not detection capability. Discarded as misleading.

**Ignoring the cross-front tension entirely.** The synthesis explicitly identified it. Leaving it unused when the ementa-as-ratio framework is directly applicable to the probe's premise would be missing the most intellectually productive move available this session. Discarded.
