## What I found

**On convergence asymmetry**: The adversarial's argument is structurally sound as stated within its own premises. If the design-relationship premise holds (art. 489 form conditions specify what correct art. 927 compliance looks like), and if design relationship implies criterion-subordination (form is derivative of authorization), then the convergence at Exit 1 is asymmetric and the convergence-case response doesn't block the inference. The form-compliant unauthorized act hypothetical is well-chosen: it correctly shows that form and authorization can diverge, and the adversarial interprets this as demonstrating that the form criterion is derivative (can exist without the primary authorization being present).

The gap I found: "can be satisfied without X" is evidence of independence from X, not derivation from X. A genuinely derivative criterion — one that cannot be satisfied without the primary — is logically incapable of being satisfied when the primary is absent. The hypothetical shows the form criterion IS satisfiable when authorization is absent. This proves independence (neither is derivative of the other), not hierarchy (form is derivative of authorization). The adversarial's inference from "form can be satisfied without authorization" to "authorization is primary" is the step that fails.

**On L2 non-autonomy**: The adversarial's argument is also structurally sound within its framing: if "saídas legítimas" is a compliance-response taxonomy, and if in compliance-response taxonomies "legítima" constitutively means "compliance-permissible," then L2 cannot operate autonomously from L1. The framing is plausible.

The gap I found: the adversarial moves from the taxonomy's subject matter (responses to an art. 927 compliance situation) to its classification criterion (compliance-permissibility). These are distinct analytical questions. A taxonomy whose subject matter is a compliance situation can classify responses by form-recognition rather than by compliance-permissibility — if the provision governing the response acts (art. 489) provides the classification framework. The adversarial must establish that subject-matter constitution by a compliance situation determines compliance-permissibility as the classification criterion. This is a substantive claim about the paper's taxonomy that requires independent support; it's not a logical entailment from "compliance-response taxonomy."

## What I decided to argue

**Independence not hierarchy**: The design-relationship premise (art. 489 tracks art. 927 for authorized exits) is accepted. What does not follow is criterion-subordination. A provision designed to specify correct compliance can independently classify unauthorized acts in ways that have their own legal significance (domain-status determination under the invalidade/incorreção distinction). Art. 489, §1º, VI generates a classification result for Exit 4 — placing departure-with-demonstration in the ordinary processing domain — regardless of authorization status. This independent significance shows criterion-independence, not derivation. The form-compliant unauthorized act hypothetical proves independence because it shows the form criterion can be satisfied without the authorization criterion — a genuinely derivative criterion cannot be satisfied without its primary.

**Joint constitution**: The taxonomy is jointly constituted by both provisions operating at different levels: art. 927 generates the compliance situation (the subject matter of the taxonomy); art. 489 provides the exit-recognition framework (the classification criterion). "Saídas" and "legítimas" derive from different provisions at different analytical levels. The adversarial assumes art. 927 organizes both; the provision-specificity argument maintains the split. Subject-matter constitution (art. 927 for the compliance situation) and classification-criterion provision (art. 489 for exit recognition) are distinct questions. Showing that the subject matter is constituted by the compliance situation doesn't establish that the classification criterion is compliance-permissibility.

**False dilemma response**: The adversarial offers a dilemma — either show "saídas legítimas" is not a compliance-response taxonomy, or show "legítima" in a compliance taxonomy means form-recognized. A third option is available and not addressed: the taxonomy IS a compliance-response taxonomy (subject matter) AND uses form-recognition as its classification criterion (organized by the provision governing response acts). These are not incompatible.

## What I considered and discarded

**Contesting the design-relationship premise directly.** I could argue that art. 489 form conditions were not designed to track art. 927 authorization — that they were designed independently to specify fundamentação requirements for a range of judicial acts. But this is not fully accurate. For Exits 1, 2, and 3, the form conditions do track the compliance obligation when correctly discharged. The design relationship is real for the authorized exits. The better response accepts the design relationship and contests the inference from design-relationship to criterion-subordination.

**Contesting the "compliance-response taxonomy" characterization.** I could argue the taxonomy is better described as a "form-classification taxonomy organized by art. 489" rather than a "compliance-response taxonomy organized by art. 927." But this is too clean. The paper does present the exits in the context of an art. 927 compliance situation. The adversarial's characterization is accurate. The better response accepts the characterization and shows it doesn't entail compliance-permissibility as the classification criterion.

**The co-extensionality argument.** I could argue that "compliance-permissible" and "form-recognized" are co-extensional in this taxonomy (for all exits except Exit 4). But this is circular — showing co-extensionality for the settled exits doesn't establish which criterion is organizing for the disputed one. It might seem to beg the question about Exit 4. Discarded as the primary argument; the independence and joint-constitution arguments are stronger.

**Conceding convergence asymmetry and focusing only on L2 autonomy.** The asymmetry argument is the adversarial's strongest advance. Conceding it while contesting L2 non-autonomy would be tempting (simpler response). But the asymmetry argument is not actually sound — the inference from independence to hierarchy fails — so conceding it would give more than the adversarial has established. Discarded.

## Assessment

The ninth ground is the adversarial's most sustained attempt to collapse the form/authorization distinction by showing the two are not in an independence relationship but in a hierarchy. The convergence-asymmetry and L2 non-autonomy arguments are connected: both rest on art. 927's compliance obligation being constitutive of the taxonomy's organizing concept, not merely its background condition.

The responses I've added establish that:
1. The design-relationship premise (accurate) doesn't entail criterion-subordination (the form criterion can be satisfied without authorization, proving independence not derivation).
2. The taxonomy's compliance-situation subject matter (accurate) doesn't entail compliance-permissibility as classification criterion (subject matter and criterion are analytically distinct).
3. A third option beyond the adversarial's dilemma is available: compliance-situation subject matter + form-recognition classification criterion.

The structural ceiling is acknowledged throughout — both readings remain coherent interpretive options, neither side has primary authority. The ninth ground reinforces the adversarial structural case and is the debate's most sophisticated advance on the criterion-which-criterion question. The responses show that the advance doesn't close the question; the dilemma the adversarial offers has a third escape.
